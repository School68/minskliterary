MINSK PAGES

Minsk. Real. Unexpected. Favorite

For Muscovites, the "bad apartment" from Mikhail Bulgakov's novel is quite real, for Londoners — the house of Sherlock Holmes, for Stockholm residents — the roof on which Carlson lived, for Veronese — the tomb of Juliet. There are also places in the Belarusian capital described in famous works.

TWO EXCURSIONS ARE WAITING FOR US HERE AT ONCE

"Famous writers on the map of Minsk" and "Minsk pages"

  • Welcome to the tour "Famous writers on the map of Minsk"! Today we will visit four wonderful places connected with the life and work of outstanding Belarusian writers.
  • Our first stop is the monument to Taras Shevchenko at the corner of Starovilenskaya and Kiseleva streets. This monument was erected in honor of one of the most famous Ukrainian poets, who is also one of the symbols of the Belarusian national culture.
  • Then we will head to the square on the City Rampart, where we will be met by a monument to Adam Mickiewicz. This Polish poet and playwright, born on the territory of modern Belarus, left a huge mark in the history of European literature
  • Monument to Alexander Sergeyevich Pushkin. This sculpture was installed in Minsk on July 10, 1999 and is a gift from the Moscow City Hall to the city of Minsk for the 200th anniversary of the birth of the great Russian poet.
  • Maxim Gorky is a great Russian writer who was very interested and loved Belarus and Belarusian literature.
  • Our next stop is the courtyard of the Belarusian State University. This is the best higher education institution in our country and it is no coincidence that there are sculptures of famous people of the planet, many of whom are Belarusian writers.
  • The Minsk Pages tour will take you on an exciting journey around Minsk and introduce you to its literary sights. Each route reveals a unique literary history.
  • Our first stop is a place associated with the chronicle "The Word about Igor's regiment". We will learn about what events took place on Nemiga and how the battle between the Rurikovichi flared up there.
  • The next stop is the house on Kuibyshev Street, 10. This is connected with another literary legend of the city - Lesya Ukrainka, a Ukrainian poet who took care of Sergei Merzhinsky, a famous revolutionary and journalist.
  • The fourth stop is connected with Vladimir Korotkevich "Chorny zamak Alshansky". Storozhevsky market
  • The fifth is Ivan Shamyakin. "Gandlyarka i paet". Komarovka
  • The sixth stop is Vincent Dunin–Marcinkevich. The poem "Vecharnitsy". Low market.
  • The seventh stop is Korney Chukovsky. "Barmaley". Engels Street. Imagine that at the beginning of the last century there was a place in Minsk that was called "Africa".

"The Word about Igor's regiment"

  • A work of ancient Russian literature that describes the campaign of Prince Igor Svyatoslavich against the Polovtsians in 1185.
  • Nemiga is one of the oldest streets in Minsk, which appeared in the XII century next to the Minsk Castle.
  • It is named after the Nemiga River, now removed into the collector, along which the street once ran.
  • In the 1960s, it was decided to demolish a significant part of the old buildings in the area of the street, cutting through a straightened wide highway
  • In 1990, the Nemiga metro station was opened, in connection with which the square occupied by the bus station on March 8 actually ceased to exist.

Bridge at Trinity suburb

  • Here we will see how the underground polonyanka flows its waters into the main city river
  • It was here, on the banks of the Svisloch River, that the legendary battle took place, in which the werewolf Prince Vseslav the Sorcerer fought
  • We will find out how Vseslav fought against his enemies and how he used his magic in battle

House on Kuibyshev str., 10

  • It is connected with another literary legend of the city - Lesya Ukrainka, a Ukrainian poet who took care of Sergei Merzhinsky, a famous revolutionary and journalist, although for ordinary people he was just a clerk of the Libavo-Romenskaya railway.
  • For two months, from January 7 to March 5, Lesya did not leave his bed until he died in her arms.
  • Sergei Merzhinsky was buried at the Strozhevsky cemetery, which was later destroyed, next to the Church of Mary Magdalene. A memorial plaque is installed on the house on Kuibyshev, 10.

Vladimir Korotkevich "Chorny zamak Alshansky". Storozhevsky market

  • Anton Kosmich, a historian and the hero of the novel, describes his house, which is located in the area with the "entrance of bachelors". The psychiatrist Lyganovsky, known as the last Prince Olshansky and, by the way, known for his malice, also lives in this residential complex.
  • Next to the house is Storozhevsky market, which was known for selling animals there.
  • This episode describes specific addresses and real prototypes. Storozhevsky market was located between Chervyakova Street and Starovilensky Tract, and now there is a memorial complex to the victims of the First World War in its place.
  • The hero of the novel, Vladimir Korotkevich, lived at Vera Khoruzhey, 48, and his friend, the famous historian and writer Adam Maldis, lived across the street at 18 Chervyakova. Interestingly, Adam Maldis is also known under the pseudonym Ales Gudas, and the real story of the treat through the window really happened.

Ivan Shamyakin. "Gandlyarka i paet".

  • The story of Olga Lenovich, a trader from Minsk, and Ales, a Red Army soldier, whom she bought out of captivity, still attracts attention. This story happened in the Komarovka area, which was not only a market, but also a residential area with a special way of life
  • It is believed that the prototype of the main character was a real trader Olga from Komarovka, whom Ivan Shamyakin noticed on the market.

Vincent Dunin–Marcinkevich. Low market.

  • One of the most famous residents of Minsk was Vincent Dunin-Marcinkevich, whose house was located on the banks of the Svisloch River, on the territory of the current National Exhibition Center.
  • In his work there were many references to urban realities, for example, in the poem "Vecharnitsy", where the narrator - grandfather Ananii - describes a Low market in which "lakatsej propast!
  • The market appeared next to the Minsk Castle, which, unfortunately, has not survived to this day, on the right bank of the Svisloch River, on the territory of the current Shopping Embankment. Subsequently, the area of the Low Market became known as the March 8 Square, and now the Nemiga metro station is located on this place.

Korney Chukovsky. "Barmaley".

  • Korney Chukovsky, who came to Minsk to give a lecture, managed to visit the "African" hospital together with Dr. Shabad.
    • It was here, in Minsk, that the idea of the evil robber Barmalei appeared, which Chukovsky described in his poem four years before writing "Doctor Aibolit".
  • Today, several dark brick buildings remain from this area, including the buildings of the city hospital on Lenin Street.

Arthur Conan Doyle. "The Adventures of Brigadier Gerard"

    Gerard, a character from The Adventures of Brigadier Gerard. Gerard, who fought in Napoleon's army, was sent on a mission to seize grain stocks stored in the Minsk City Hall.
  • Upon arrival in the city, Gerard intercepted the captain of the Grodno dragoons, whose secret dispatch was translated for him by the daughter of a local priest named Sophie.
  • She warned Gerard that everything was lost if the French came to Minsk. But Gerard was delighted with this information and led his squad on the offensive.
